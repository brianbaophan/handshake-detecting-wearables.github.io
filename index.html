Our Project

Social networking in the industry is one of the major keys to being successful. When meeting someone for the first time, a common mistake is forgetting to distribute a business card. This could be devastating because potential business connections could be lost.

We wanted to create a system that assured that individuals would make a connection right when they met someone new. For our Senior Project, we created a wearable device that detects when two individuals shake hands. After a handshake is detected between two users who have been registered into our database, an email is sent to each user. The email contains the contact information from the person who they have just met.

Hardware Used

Two main components were used as parameters to detect handshakes, contact and motion. Contact between the hands of two users with designated RFID tags was identified using a PN532 Near-Field Communication Controller and motion was detected using a ADXL345 3-Axis accelerometer. The hardware components notifies the processor a handshake is detected in which the processor for our wearables is Laird’s BL652 development board. The processor would then notify our central device, the Raspberry Pi via Bluetooth Low Energy, to perform a user information distributing task.

End Result

Once the correct user information is fetched by the main server, a message is generated and sent via email to the designated user. The server would repeat the task for the other user for additional handshakes.
